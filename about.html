About RayCo

Rayna Milkova is the founder of RayCo, a business intelligence consultancy, based in London. With over 20 years in the industry, Rayna specialises in complex, multi-jurisdictional investigations, including litigation support and asset tracing, as well as due diligence and anti-financial crime concerns, such as money laundering, bribery, corruption, and sanctions.

She has also conducted in-depth assignments looking into sources of wealth, political exposure and reputational issues for clients, including corporations, financial institutions, law firms and HNW individuals.

Rayna has extensive experience liaising with the media and various regulators, as well as key internal and external stakeholders.

Most recently, she worked at Schillings, a top UK reputation management law firm, where she led its business intelligence team.

Prior to that, for over six years, she led the Russia and CIS team in the Deutsche Bank’s Anti-Financial Crime unit, providing advisory services across key international hubs and banking divisions. She also spent time in the Deutsche Bank's Hong Kong office, working on the APAC client portfolio between 2017 and 2018.

Before joining Deutsche Bank, Rayna worked for over 15 years in top business intelligence firms, such as Kroll and GPW, overseeing extensive due diligence assignments, as well as investigations in Russia and the CIS, Europe, the US, Africa and the Far East for a variety of clients, including banks, financial institutions and multinational corporations.

Rayna has a wide network of developed sources in these jurisdictions across a range of industries, including telecoms, commodities, finance and law, that she can call upon for confidential advice and information. She also has an in-depth knowledge of open sources that includes not just what is publicly available, but also what can be accessed but is not easily obtainable.

She is a dual UK and EU national, and speaks fluent English, Russian and Bulgarian, and has a working knowledge of French, as well as basic German and Italian.
